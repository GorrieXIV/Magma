Introduction








In this chapter we will present the categories of finite
projective and affine planes. 


The category names for projective and affine planes are PlaneProj
and PlaneAff respectively. Within each of these categories we have what
we will call classical planes --- those which are defined by a 
vector space of dimension 2 (for affine planes) or 3 (for projective
planes).


Some functions documented here apply to all types of planes, others
are specific to projective, affine or classical planes.
It should be clear which is the case for each entry.

Planes in Magma








A point of a plane is considered to be a special object, and so
points are given their own special type, PlanePt, in Magma.
This allows the points of a plane to be defined over any type of
Magma object, and also improves the efficiency of the code.


A special structure called the point--set acts as the parent structure
for points.  A point is created by coercing an appropriate Magma
object into the point--set. It is also possible the get the i-th point,
or a random point, from the point--set.


Similarly, lines of a plane have a special type PlaneLn, and
the line--set acts as their parent structure.
Lines can be created by coercing a suitable object into the line--set,
or by asking for the i-th line, or a random line, from the line--set.
